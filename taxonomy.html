The "Data" ‚Äî Taxonomy & Labelling Strategy

This is where 90% of AI projects fail. If you tell the AI to sort emails into "General Inquiries," it will fail because everything is a general inquiry.

The Overlooked PM Skill

Every ML engineer will tell you: "Garbage in, garbage out." But what they won't tell you is that defining the "garbage" is a product decision, not a technical one.

You need distinct, non-overlapping categories (classes). The moment two categories can reasonably contain the same email, your model will struggle and your agents will lose trust.

üìö Parallel Example

Library Systems

You don't just have a "Books" section. You have "Fiction," "Non-Fiction," "Sci-Fi," "Biography." A book cannot easily be in two places at once. The Dewey Decimal System works because it forces mutual exclusivity. Your email taxonomy must do the same.

The Golden Rule

Your categories must be distinct enough that a 5-year-old could tell them apart. If you have to explain the difference between two categories, they're too similar.

‚ö†Ô∏è Common Anti-Pattern

"Shipping Question" and "Where is my Order?" ‚Äî These feel different but are functionally identical. The AI will constantly confuse them, and your accuracy metrics will suffer. Merge them into one category: "Order Status & Delivery."

‚úì The Fix

Ask yourself: "Would the same support agent handle both of these?" If yes, they should be the same category. Categories should map to routing decisions, not semantic meanings.

The 4 Intent Categories

For an e-commerce store (e.g., selling shoes) ‚Äî distinct, actionable, and mutually exclusive.

Returns & Refunds

Customer wants money back or to exchange a product. High urgency, clear resolution path.

Signal Keywords
"refund" "return" "money back" "exchange" "wrong size"
üì¶

Order Status & Delivery

Where is my order? When will it arrive? Tracking inquiries. Low complexity, high volume.

Signal Keywords
"tracking" "delivery" "shipped" "ETA" "where is"
üëü

Product Questions

Pre-sale inquiries. Sizing, materials, availability. Opportunity for conversion.

Signal Keywords
"size guide" "available in" "what material" "restock" "does it fit"
üîê

Account & Technical

Login issues, password resets, payment problems. Requires system access to resolve.

Signal Keywords
"can't login" "password" "payment failed" "update email" "account"

Why These 4?

Returns & Refunds: Different team, different SLA, different authority level. An agent needs refund approval rights to handle these.

Order Status: 60% of all e-commerce emails. Automatable. Often resolved by sending a tracking link‚Äîno human needed.

Product Questions: Pre-sale = revenue opportunity. These should go to sales-trained agents, not support agents.

Account & Technical: Requires backend access. These agents need different tools than the refund team.
