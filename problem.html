The "Why" ‚Äî The Operational Bottleneck

In AI for Operations, we don't solve "user happiness" directly‚Äîwe solve inefficiency. Defining the pain point shapes every technical decision downstream.

The Core Insight

Customer Support Agents (CSAs) are expensive humans. Right now, we're paying them to read an email and decide "This is a Refund Request." That's a waste of human intelligence.

The same agent who could be crafting a thoughtful response to an angry customer, building loyalty and preventing churn, is instead doing cognitive sorting work that a machine should handle.

üìÆ Parallel Example

The Post Office

They don't have humans reading every zip code on every envelope anymore. Machines read the zip code and sort the mail. Humans only touch the mail that has bad handwriting. Our AI email system follows the same principle‚Äîautomate the obvious, escalate the ambiguous.

Framing the Problem Statement

Before we write a single line of code or train any model, we need absolute clarity on what we're optimizing for. The answer to this question determines:

Model Aggressiveness: How confident should the AI be before it acts autonomously?

Success Metrics: What numbers will we show leadership in the quarterly review?

Trade-off Decisions: When speed and accuracy conflict, which wins?

The Key Decision

Which specific pain point are we solving for the Support Manager?

‚úì
Option A

Speed

Customers wait 24+ hours for a reply. First response time is killing our CSAT scores.

‚úì
Option B

Cost

We're hiring too many agents just to sort mail. Headcount is growing faster than revenue.

‚úì
Option C

Accuracy

Tired agents send "Refund" tickets to the "Tech Support" team by mistake. Misrouting is chaos.

üí° Why Cost?

For an MVP, Cost is the most defensible choice. It's directly measurable (headcount, $/ticket), easy to prove ROI to leadership, and gives us room to iterate. If we pick Speed, we're racing against infrastructure investments. If we pick Accuracy, we need near-perfect models before launch. Cost lets us demonstrate value even with an 80% automation rate.

The Implications of Our Choice

By choosing Cost Reduction as our primary pain point, we've made several implicit decisions:

Model Strategy: We'll prioritize high-confidence classifications. It's better to leave an email for a human than to misroute it.

Success Metric: Our North Star becomes "Tickets auto-classified without human intervention."

MVP Scope: We can launch with fewer intent categories. Even 4 well-defined buckets can drive significant value.
